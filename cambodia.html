Ancient Temples

Angkor Wat

The magnificent 12th-century temple complex, the largest religious monument in the world. A UNESCO World Heritage site and symbol of Cambodia.

Bayon Temple

Famous for its massive stone faces, this temple sits at the center of Angkor Thom. Each face represents Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva of compassion.

Ta Prohm

Known as the "Tomb Raider Temple," this ancient temple is being reclaimed by massive tree roots, creating a mystical atmosphere.

Khmer Culture

Apsara Dance

Traditional Khmer classical dance performed by graceful dancers in elaborate costumes, telling stories from ancient Hindu epics.

Water Festival

Bon Om Touk, Cambodia's most important festival, celebrating the reversal of the Mekong River with dragon boat races and festivities.

Traditional Crafts

Beautiful handmade silk textiles, silver jewelry, and wood carvings showcasing centuries-old Khmer artistic traditions.

Natural Beauty

Mekong River

The mighty Mekong flows through Cambodia, providing life to millions and home to the rare Irrawaddy dolphins.

Coastal Beaches

Pristine beaches along the Gulf of Thailand, including Sihanoukville and the peaceful islands of Koh Rong.

Cardamom Mountains

Lush rainforests home to endangered species like Asian elephants, sun bears, and over 450 bird species.

Delicious Cuisine

Amok

Cambodia's national dish - fragrant fish curry steamed in banana leaves with coconut milk and aromatic spices.

Kuy Teav

Popular breakfast noodle soup with pork or beef, served with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and lime.

Fresh Tropical Fruits

Exotic fruits like dragon fruit, rambutan, and the world's best mangoes grow abundantly in Cambodia's tropical climate.

About the Kingdom of Cambodia

Cambodia, officially known as the Kingdom of Cambodia, is a country in Southeast Asia known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and warm hospitality. Home to the magnificent Angkor temple complex, Cambodia has a fascinating culture that blends ancient traditions with modern life.

The country is famous for the Khmer Empire, which flourished from the 9th to 15th centuries and left behind incredible architectural marvels. Today, Cambodia is a peaceful nation working to preserve its cultural heritage while embracing development and tourism.

UNESCO Sites

Cambodia has 3 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the famous Angkor Archaeological Park.

National Flower

The Rumduol (a type of banana flower) is Cambodia's national flower, symbolizing the gentle nature of Khmer women.

Language

Khmer is the official language, spoken by over 95% of the population. It has its own unique alphabet with 74 letters.
